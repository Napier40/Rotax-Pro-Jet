Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Rotax Pro Jet

Rotax Pro Jet is a web application designed to help Rotax kart racers optimize their carburetor jetting based on environmental conditions. It uses mathematical models to calculate the ideal main jet size, needle position, and float height for your specific conditions.

Our calculations are based on the research from Bowmain Consulting on Rotax carburetor jetting, providing scientifically accurate recommendations that take the guesswork out of jetting.

Rotax Pro Jet currently supports the following Rotax MAX kart engines:

  • Senior MAX EVO
  • Junior MAX EVO
  • Mini MAX

We're continuously working to add support for more engine variants. If you need support for a specific engine that's not listed, please contact us.

Our jetting recommendations are based on scientific calculations and have been validated through extensive track testing. However, they should be used as a starting point for your setup.

Factors such as engine condition, exhaust system, and driving style can influence the optimal jetting. We recommend verifying our recommendations by checking spark plug color and monitoring engine performance.

Many users report that our recommendations are spot-on, while others may need to make minor adjustments based on their specific setup.

No special equipment is needed! Rotax Pro Jet works on any modern web browser and can be accessed from your smartphone, tablet, or computer.

You don't need weather instruments or measuring tools, as our application can automatically fetch weather data for your location. However, if you prefer, you can also manually enter temperature, pressure, and humidity values from your own instruments.

Our jetting calculator uses the following formula derived from Bowmain Consulting's research:

j₂ = j₁ × (ρ₂/ρ₁)^(1/4)

Where:

  • j₂ is the recommended jet size
  • j₁ is the reference jet size
  • ρ₂ is the current air density
  • ρ₁ is the reference air density

Air density is calculated based on temperature, pressure, and humidity using standard atmospheric equations. The calculator then applies additional algorithms to determine the optimal needle position and float height.

Reference conditions are the baseline environmental conditions and jet size that you know work well for your engine. By default, we use the Rotax standard reference conditions:

  • Temperature: 25°C (77°F)
  • Pressure: 990 hPa
  • Humidity: 0%
  • Air density: 1.157 kg/m³

Advanced users can set custom reference conditions if they have a known good setup for specific conditions. This can help fine-tune the recommendations for your particular engine.

The best way to verify if the recommended jet is correct is to check your spark plug color after a full-throttle run:

  • Light tan color: Optimal mixture
  • Black or sooty: Mixture is too rich (larger jet than needed)
  • White or light gray: Mixture is too lean (smaller jet than needed)

You should also pay attention to engine performance. A properly jetted engine will have crisp throttle response, good acceleration, and consistent top-end power. If the engine hesitates, bogs down, or overheats, the jetting may need adjustment.

We use reliable weather data providers to ensure the most accurate information possible. However, weather conditions can vary significantly even within short distances, especially at racetracks with varying elevation or near bodies of water.

For the most accurate results, we recommend:

  • Using the "Current Location" feature when you're physically at the track
  • Taking measurements at the specific area of the track where you'll be racing
  • Updating the weather data periodically throughout the day if conditions are changing

If you have your own weather instruments, you can also manually enter the values for maximum accuracy.
